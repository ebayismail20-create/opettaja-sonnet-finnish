üìö Grammar Focus: The Alphabet & Stress

Rule 1: Word Stress
In Finnish, the stress (emphasis) is ALWAYS on the first syllable. ALWAYS.
Examples: SUO-mi, KII-tos, AN-teek-si.

Rule 2: The "Dots" (√Ñ and √ñ)
A vs √Ñ: A is back (like "father"). √Ñ is front (like "cat").
O vs √ñ: O is back (like "hot"). √ñ is front (like "fur" or "earl" but lips rounded).
Y: Pronounced like "ew" in "new" but with very rounded lips.

Rule 3: Double Letters = Long Sound
Single letter is short, double letter is long (2x duration).
Tuli (fire) vs Tuuli (wind). This changes meaning!
üß† Easy Way to Remember (Vowel Harmony):
Think of your mouth as a house.
Back vowels (A, O, U) live in the back (throat).
Front vowels (√Ñ, √ñ, Y) live in the front (teeth).
Neutral vowels (E, I) can visit anyone.
You usually can't mix Back and Front vowels in one word!
